# Voice & Persona System

## Why Voice Matters

Same topic, same framework, same platform — but different voices produce completely different content. Voice is your moat. It's the reason readers follow _you_ instead of reading anyone else.

---

## Voice Profile Template

Before writing, lock down these 5 dimensions:

### 1. Identity (我是谁)

```
Background:  {profession, experience, key credentials}
Stance:      {what do you believe that most people don't?}
Superpower:  {what unique perspective or access do you have?}
```

Example:

- "Ex-Google engineer, now solo founder. Believes most tech career advice is survivorship bias."
- "95后留学生，在美国大厂干了3年又回国创业。觉得海归光环是个幻觉。"

### 2. Tone Spectrum (语气光谱)

Place yourself on each axis:

```
Formal ←————————→ Casual
Serious ←————————→ Playful
Authoritative ←——→ Vulnerable
Optimistic ←—————→ Skeptical
Polished ←————————→ Raw
```

Most strong voices are NOT in the middle. Pick a side.

Example profiles:

- **Tech 毒舌**: Casual + Playful + Authoritative + Skeptical + Raw
- **温暖导师**: Casual + Serious + Vulnerable + Optimistic + Polished
- **学术型博主**: Formal + Serious + Authoritative + Skeptical + Polished
- **朋友型分享**: Casual + Playful + Vulnerable + Optimistic + Raw

### 3. Signature Moves (标志性动作)

Every recognizable voice has recurring patterns readers come to expect:

| Type            | Examples                                                          |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Catchphrase     | "说句大实话" / "Here's the thing nobody tells you"                |
| Structure habit | Always opens with a scene / Always uses numbered lists            |
| Emotional beat  | Always self-deprecates before making a strong point               |
| Closing style   | Always ends with a question / Always ends with a one-liner        |
| Language quirk  | Mixes English into Chinese / Uses parenthetical asides constantly |

Pick 2-3 signature moves. Use them consistently. This is how readers recognize your "fingerprint."

### 4. Vocabulary DNA (词汇基因)

**Words you always use:**

- List 5-10 words/phrases that feel like "you"
- These become your voice markers

**Words you never use:**

- List words that feel wrong for your persona
- Typically includes AI-smell words, but also words that don't match your tone

Example for a casual tech voice:

- Always: 搞、折腾、踩坑、真相是、说白了
- Never: 值得注意的是、不言而喻、至关重要、赋能

### 5. Reader Relationship (和读者的关系)

| Relationship               | Voice Implication                                 |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Mentor → Student           | Can be directive, share lessons, say "you should" |
| Friend → Friend            | Equal footing, "我也不确定", share doubts         |
| Insider → Outsider         | Reveal secrets, "they don't want you to know"     |
| Explorer → Fellow explorer | "I'm figuring this out too, here's what I found"  |
| Expert → Practitioner      | Technical depth OK, skip basics, peer respect     |

Pick ONE primary relationship. Mixing them feels inconsistent.

## Voice Consistency Check

After drafting, verify voice consistency:

1. **Pronoun test**: Is the I/you/we ratio consistent throughout? A sudden shift from "我" to "人们" breaks voice.
2. **Tone drift test**: Read the first paragraph and the last. Do they sound like the same person?
3. **Vocabulary scan**: Search for any words from your "never use" list.
4. **Relationship test**: Are you a mentor in paragraph 1 and a friend in paragraph 5? Pick one.
5. **Signature check**: Did you use at least 1 of your signature moves?

## Adapting Voice Across Platforms

Your core voice stays the same. Only the **intensity** changes:

| Platform   | Adjustment                                                        |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| X/Twitter  | Sharpen the edges. More punchy, more opinionated.                 |
| 公众号     | More depth, more storytelling. Slightly warmer.                   |
| LinkedIn   | Same opinions, more professional framing. Less slang.             |
| 小红书     | More casual, more emoji, more 闺蜜 energy.                        |
| Newsletter | Most authentic. Readers opted in — you can be most yourself here. |

The key: a reader who follows you on X and subscribes to your newsletter should recognize the same person, just in different settings — like how you talk differently at a bar vs. a conference, but you're still you.

## Quick Start

If the user hasn't defined their voice yet, ask these 3 questions:

1. "你平时发朋友圈/微博是什么风格？" — This reveals natural voice better than any questionnaire.
2. "有没有你特别喜欢的博主/作者？哪一点像你想要的风格？" — Gives a reference anchor.
3. "你的读者是谁？你和他们是什么关系？" — Locks down the relationship axis.

From these 3 answers, draft a Voice Profile and confirm with the user before writing.
